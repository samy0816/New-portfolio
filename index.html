About Me


I like making technology feel less like “technology” and more like something you just get. My background’s in Computer Engineering, but along the way I got hooked on the human side of things why some products feel effortless while others feel like a fight. That curiosity is what brought me to Saarland University, where I’m doing my MSc in Informatics and spending a lot of time exploring human–computer interaction. Most of my projects start the same way: a vague idea, a pile of data, or a problem that hasn’t been solved yet. I enjoy untangling all that, figuring out what really matters, and building something that’s clear, quick, and easy to use. No overcomplication, no fluff—just design and systems that quietly do their job, so people don’t have to think about the tech at all.
